### Recipe 14.1: One-Pan Lemon Chicken with Roasted Potatoes

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 | Boneless skinless chicken breasts (5-6 oz each) | 🟢 |
| 4 cups | Yukon Gold potatoes, diced into 1-inch cubes | 🟢 |
| 3 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried rosemary | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried thyme | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Green beans, trimmed | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, combine diced potatoes with 1½ tablespoons olive oil, rosemary, thyme, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon pepper. Toss to coat.
3. Spread potatoes in a single layer on half of the baking sheet. Roast for 10 minutes.
4. While potatoes roast, season chicken breasts with remaining salt and pepper. In the same bowl, combine remaining olive oil and lemon juice. Add chicken and turn to coat.
5. Remove baking sheet from oven. Place chicken on the other half of the sheet. Return to oven and roast 12 minutes.
6. Scatter green beans around the chicken and potatoes. Roast another 5-8 minutes, until chicken reaches 165°F internal and potatoes are golden and tender.
7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash and scrub the potatoes, toss the potatoes in oil and herbs, and snip the parsley with scissors.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Chicken breasts of uneven thickness will cook unevenly. Pound thicker ends to an even thickness with a rolling pin or heavy skillet before seasoning. Or use chicken thighs — they're more forgiving and stay juicier.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 14.2: Quick Beef & Vegetable Skillet

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Ground beef (85/15) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Zucchini, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Carrots, diced | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh ginger, grated | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Sesame seeds | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Cooked white rice (for serving) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon, until browned (5-6 minutes).
2. Remove beef with a slotted spoon to a plate, leaving 1 tablespoon of drippings in the pan.
3. Add zucchini, bell pepper, and carrots to the same skillet. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until crisp-tender.
4. Return the beef to the skillet. Add tamari and grated ginger. Stir to combine and cook 2 minutes.
5. Remove from heat. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
6. Serve over cooked white rice.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the rice and rinse it before cooking, and sprinkle the sesame seeds on the finished dish.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Grate fresh ginger with a microplane or the small side of a box grater — the fibrous strings stay behind in the grater and you get only the juicy pulp. Freeze a whole ginger root; it grates easily without peeling when frozen.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use tamari, not soy sauce) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 14.3: Low-FODMAP "Spaghetti" Bolognese

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Ground beef or ground turkey | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 3 medium | Carrots, finely diced (substitute for onion) | 🟢 |
| 2 stalks | Celery (outer stalks), finely diced | 🟢 |
| 2 cans (15 oz each) | Crushed tomatoes (no garlic/onion added) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Dried basil | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Sugar | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 12 oz | Gluten-free spaghetti (brown rice or corn-based) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh basil, thinly sliced (for garnish) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Parmesan cheese, grated (optional) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil for the pasta.
2. While water heats, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground beef and cook, breaking apart, until browned (5 minutes).
3. Add diced carrots and celery to the skillet. Cook 5 minutes, stirring, until softened.
4. Add crushed tomatoes, dried basil, oregano, sugar, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine. Reduce heat to low and simmer 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
5. Cook gluten-free pasta according to package directions. Drain well.
6. Serve sauce over pasta. Garnish with fresh basil and parmesan if using.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child use a food processor (with supervision) to pulse the carrots and celery into an onion-like texture. They can also add the herbs and stir the sauce.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** The finely diced carrots and celery mimic the texture of onion in the sauce without triggering FODMAP symptoms. This is the single most important substitution to master — it works in nearly any recipe that calls for onion as a flavor base.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF pasta) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes when omitting parmesan |

### Recipe 14.4: 15-Minute Shrimp & Zucchini Skillet

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Raw shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails optional) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 medium | Zucchini, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh parsley, chopped | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Cooked quinoa or rice (for serving) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Pat shrimp dry with paper towels. Season with ¼ teaspoon salt and a pinch of pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
3. Add shrimp in a single layer. Cook 2 minutes per side, until pink and opaque. Remove to a plate.
4. In the same skillet, add zucchini and bell pepper. Cook 4 minutes, stirring, until crisp-tender.
5. Return shrimp to the skillet. Add lemon juice, oregano, remaining salt, and pepper. Toss to combine and cook 1 minute.
6. Remove from heat. Garnish with parsley. Serve over quinoa or rice.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child remove the tails from the shrimp (if not already done), squeeze the lemon juice, and arrange the shrimp on the serving plate.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Shrimp cook in minutes and are ruined by overcooking (they turn rubbery). As soon as they curl into a C shape and turn pink, they're done. An O shape means overcooked. Buy frozen raw shrimp and thaw in a bowl of cold water for 10 minutes.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 14.5: Turkey & Spinach Rice Bowls

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Ground turkey | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Low-FODMAP chicken broth | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Fresh baby spinach | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Cherry tomatoes, halved | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Paprika | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Cooked white or brown rice (for serving) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground turkey and cook, breaking apart, until browned (5-6 minutes).
2. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika. Stir to combine.
3. Add chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Cook 3 minutes, scraping up any browned bits.
4. Add spinach and cherry tomatoes. Cook 2-3 minutes, stirring, until spinach is wilted and tomatoes soften slightly.
5. Remove from heat. Stir in lemon juice.
6. Serve over rice in bowls.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash the spinach in a colander, halve the cherry tomatoes with a butter knife, and scoop the rice into bowls.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Ground turkey dries out faster than ground beef. Adding a splash of broth and not overcooking keeps it moist. If using ground beef instead, skip the broth step.

### Recipe 14.6: Sheet Pan Salmon with Roasted Veggies

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 18 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 | Salmon fillets (5-6 oz each) | 🟢 |
| 3 cups | Broccoli florets (heads only) | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Cherry tomatoes | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Red bell pepper, sliced into strips | 🟢 |
| 3 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried dill | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| Lemon wedges (for serving) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, combine broccoli, cherry tomatoes, and bell pepper. Drizzle with 1½ tablespoons olive oil, sprinkle with ½ teaspoon salt and ¼ teaspoon pepper. Toss to coat.

4. While vegetables roast, pat salmon fillets dry. Brush with remaining olive oil and lemon juice. Season with dill, remaining salt and pepper.
5. Remove baking sheet from oven. Place salmon fillets skin-side down on the other half of the sheet.
6. Return to oven and roast 10-12 minutes, until salmon flakes easily with a fork and vegetables are tender.
7. Serve with lemon wedges.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child arrange the vegetables on the baking sheet, brush the salmon with oil, and squeeze the lemon wedges at the table.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Salmon with the skin on is more forgiving — the skin acts as a barrier against overcooking. If your child is put off by skin, it peels off easily after cooking.

### Recipe 15.1: Slow Cooker Chicken & Vegetable Stew

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 6-8 hours (low) or 3-4 hours (high)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1½ lbs | Boneless skinless chicken thighs | 🟢 |
| 3 medium | Carrots, peeled and sliced | 🟢 |
| 3 stalks | Celery (outer stalks), sliced | 🟢 |
| 2 medium | Yukon Gold potatoes, diced into 1-inch cubes | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| 4 cups | Low-FODMAP chicken broth | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried thyme | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried rosemary | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh parsley, chopped | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Place chicken thighs at the bottom of the slow cooker.
2. Add carrots, celery, potatoes, and bell pepper on top.
3. Pour in the low-FODMAP chicken broth. Add thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Do not stir — layering keeps the chicken submerged and prevents drying out.
4. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours.
5. Remove chicken thighs, shred with two forks, and return to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash and scrub the potatoes and carrots, measure the herbs, and add ingredients to the slow cooker (supervised — the pot is heavy).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Chicken thighs stay juicier than breasts during long slow cooking. If you prefer breasts, add them halfway through cooking (after 3-4 hours on low) to prevent drying out.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 15.2: One-Pot Creamy Chicken & Rice

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Boneless skinless chicken breasts, diced into 1-inch pieces | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 medium | Carrots, diced | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | White rice (uncooked) | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Low-FODMAP chicken broth | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free whole milk | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Frozen peas | 🟡 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Dried thyme | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives, snipped | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. 
2. Add diced chicken and cook 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden on all sides. Remove to a plate.
3. In the same pot, add carrots and cook 3 minutes, stirring.
4. Add the rice and stir to coat in the oil, cooking 1 minute.
5. Pour in the chicken broth and milk. Add salt, pepper, and thyme. Stir, then bring to a boil.
6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 18-20 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is mostly absorbed.
7. Return the chicken to the pot. Add frozen peas. Stir, cover, and cook 3 more minutes.
8. Remove from heat. Let rest 5 minutes (the residual heat finishes the cooking). Garnish with chives.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the rice and broth, add ingredients to the pot, and snip the chives.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Peas are a yellow-light food — ¼ cup per serving is safe. This recipe uses ½ cup total across 4 servings, which is well within the limit. If your child is extra sensitive, omit the peas and add extra diced carrot instead.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (¼ cup peas per serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use extra broth instead of milk, or use oat milk) |

### Recipe 15.3: Slow Cooker Beef & Potato Casserole

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 hours (low)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 lbs | Beef chuck roast, cut into 1½-inch cubes | 🟢 |
| 4 medium | Yukon Gold potatoes, sliced into ¼-inch rounds | 🟢 |
| 3 medium | Carrots, sliced into ½-inch rounds | 🟢 |
| 2 stalks | Celery (outer stalks), sliced | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Low-FODMAP beef broth | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Tomato paste (check label — no garlic/onion) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried thyme | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried rosemary | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water (slurry) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh parsley, chopped | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Pat beef cubes dry with paper towels. Season with half the salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat. Sear beef in batches (don't crowd the pan) for 3-4 minutes per side until deeply browned. Transfer to the slow cooker.
3. To the same skillet, add ½ cup beef broth and scrape up the browned bits with a wooden spoon. Pour this liquid into the slow cooker.
4. Add potatoes, carrots, and celery to the slow cooker on top of the beef.
5. In a small bowl, whisk together remaining beef broth, tomato paste, tamari, thyme, rosemary, and remaining salt and pepper. Pour over the beef and vegetables.
6. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours (or high for 4-5 hours).
7. In the last 15 minutes, stir in the cornstarch slurry to thicken the gravy. Cover and cook 15 minutes.
8. Garnish with parsley and serve.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child arrange the potato and carrot slices in the slow cooker, measure the herbs, and stir the cornstarch slurry.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Searing the beef before slow cooking is non-negotiable for flavor. The brown crust (Maillard reaction) is where deep, savory flavor comes from. Skipping this step gives you gray, bland meat.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 15.4: One-Pot Low-FODMAP "Chili" (No Beans)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 35 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Ground beef | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 medium | Carrots, finely diced | 🟢 |
| 2 stalks | Celery (outer stalks), finely diced | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| 2 cans (15 oz each) | Diced tomatoes (no garlic/onion added) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Low-FODMAP beef or chicken broth | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Tomato paste | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Chili powder (check label — no garlic/onion powder) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Cumin | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Paprika | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |

### Recipe 15.5: One-Pot Lemon Herb Fish & Potatoes

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 25 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

### Recipe 15.6: Slow Cooker Pulled Chicken (for Tacos, Wraps & Bowls)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 6 hours (low) or 3 hours (high)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6-8 servings (freeze half)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 lbs | Boneless skinless chicken breasts or thighs | 🟢 |
| 1 can (15 oz) | Diced tomatoes, drained (no garlic/onion) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Tomato paste | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Chili powder (no garlic/onion) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Cumin | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Paprika | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Low-FODMAP chicken broth | 🟢 |

**For serving (choose):**
- Corn tortillas 🟢
- Lettuce wraps 🟢
- Rice bowls 🟢
- Bell pepper slices 🟢

**Toppings (choose):**
- Shredded cheddar cheese 🟢
- Lactose-free sour cream 🟢
- Sliced avocado (⅛ per serving) 🟡
- Fresh cilantro 🟢
- Lime wedges 🟢

**Instructions:**

1. Place chicken in the slow cooker in a single layer.
2. In a medium bowl, combine diced tomatoes, tomato paste, olive oil, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir to form a sauce.
3. Pour the sauce over the chicken, turning to coat each piece.
4. Pour chicken broth around the edges (not directly on top — you don't want to wash off the seasoning).
5. Cover and cook on low for 6 hours or high for 3 hours.
6. Remove chicken to a cutting board. Shred with two forks. Return to the slow cooker and stir into the sauce.
7. Let sit 10 minutes to absorb the sauce.
8. Serve in corn tortillas, lettuce wraps, or over rice with desired toppings.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the spices, stir the sauce together, and shred the cooked chicken with two forks.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Freeze half of this pulled chicken in a zip-top bag with the sauce. Thaw in the fridge overnight and reheat for a 5-minute dinner on a future busy night. This is the ultimate meal prep staple.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use corn tortillas or rice) |

### Recipe 16.2: Lemon Green Beans with Sesame

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Fresh green beans, trimmed | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Sesame oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Sesame seeds | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add green beans and cook 3 minutes until crisp-tender.
2. Drain and immediately transfer to a bowl of ice water to stop cooking (30 seconds). Drain well.
3. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, combine olive oil and sesame oil.
4. Add green beans and cook 2 minutes, stirring, until heated through.
5. Remove from heat. Toss with lemon juice, salt, and sesame seeds. Serve.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child snap the ends off the green beans (this is surprisingly satisfying for kids) and sprinkle the sesame seeds.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** The ice bath step is optional but keeps the beans bright green and crisp. If you're serving immediately, you can skip it and go straight from boiling to the skillet.

### Recipe 16.3: Crispy Rosemary Roasted Potatoes

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 lbs | Yukon Gold potatoes, diced into 1-inch cubes | 🟢 |
| 3 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh rosemary, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Place a large rimmed baking sheet in the oven while it preheats (hot pan = crispy potatoes).
2. In a large bowl, toss potato cubes with olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
3. Carefully remove the hot baking sheet from the oven. Spread potatoes in a single layer (they should sizzle when they hit the pan).
4. Roast 25-30 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until golden brown and crispy outside, tender inside.
5. Serve immediately.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child scrub the potatoes, toss them in oil and herbs, and spread them on the baking sheet (adult handles the hot pan).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** The hot-pan trick is the secret to crispy roasted potatoes. Cold pan = steamed potatoes. Hot pan = seared, crispy potatoes. Yukon Golds have the best balance of starch and waxiness for roasting.

### Recipe 16.4: Sesame Roasted Bok Choy

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 heads | Baby bok choy, halved lengthwise | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Sesame oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Fresh ginger, grated | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Sesame seeds | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Place bok choy halves cut-side up on the baking sheet.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together sesame oil, tamari, and grated ginger.
4. Brush the mixture over the bok choy halves, making sure to get some between the leaves.
5. Roast 8-10 minutes, until bok choy is tender and the edges are slightly crispy.
6. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child brush the oil mixture onto the bok choy and sprinkle the sesame seeds.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Bok choy is a cruciferous vegetable that's actually LOW in FODMAPs (unlike cauliflower, broccoli stems, and cabbage which are high). It's mild-flavored and slightly sweet when roasted — many kids who "don't like vegetables" will eat this.

### Recipe 16.5: Bell Pepper & Zucchini Sauté

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 medium | Zucchini, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Yellow bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh basil, thinly sliced | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Lemon zest | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add zucchini and bell peppers. Cook 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until tender-crisp and starting to brown at the edges.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash the vegetables, remove the seeds from the bell peppers, and tear the basil leaves.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Removing the seeds from zucchini prevents the sauté from becoming watery. Halve the zucchini lengthwise and run a spoon down the center to scoop out the seeds before dicing.

### Recipe 16.6: Simple Tomato & Cucumber Salad

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 cups | Cherry tomatoes, halved | 🟢 |
| 1½ cups | English cucumber, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Red wine vinegar | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh basil or parsley, chopped | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a medium bowl, combine cherry tomatoes and cucumber.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
3. Pour dressing over the vegetables. Toss gently.
4. Fold in fresh herbs. Serve immediately or refrigerate up to 2 hours.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child halve the cherry tomatoes with a butter knife, scoop the cucumber seeds, and whisk the dressing.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** English cucumbers have thinner skins and fewer seeds than regular cucumbers — they're worth the slightly higher price for kid-friendly salads. If using regular cucumbers, peel them and scoop out the seeds.
