## Chapter 9: Quick Morning Meals

*School mornings are chaos. We know. These recipes come together in 20 minutes or less — no blender required for most, minimal cleanup, and built from pantry staples you'll always have on hand.*

### Recipe 9.1: Banana Oat Pancakes

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 8-10 small pancakes (serves 2 kids or 1 adult + 1 kid)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 medium | Bananas (firm, yellow — not overripe) | 🟢 |
| 3 large | Eggs | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Gluten-free rolled oats, blended into flour | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Butter (for cooking) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Blueberries (optional, for topping) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Maple syrup (for serving) | 🟡 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a medium bowl, mash the bananas with a fork until smooth with a few small lumps remaining.
2. Crack the eggs into the bowl and whisk into the banana until fully combined.
3. Add the oat flour and cinnamon. Stir until no dry spots remain. The batter will be thinner than regular pancake batter — that's correct.
4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add butter and let it melt, tilting the pan to coat the surface.
5. Pour roughly 2 tablespoons of batter per pancake onto the hot surface. Leave space between pancakes — they'll spread slightly.
6. Cook for 2-3 minutes until bubbles form across the surface and edges look set. Flip and cook 1-2 minutes on the other side.
7. Serve immediately with blueberries and a drizzle of maple syrup.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child mash the bananas with the fork and stir the batter. For younger kids, let them place blueberries onto the pancakes after you flip them.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Overripe bananas (brown spots) are higher in fructans — use firm yellow bananas for the safest FODMAP profile. Freeze overripe bananas for smoothies where the smaller serving dilutes the FODMAP load.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at 4-5 pancakes per serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (sub butter with coconut oil or ghee) |

### Recipe 9.2: Cheesy Scrambled Eggs with Spinach

**Prep Time:** 3 minutes | **Cook Time:** 7 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** Serves 2 kids or 1 adult + 1 kid

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 large | Eggs | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Butter | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh baby spinach, roughly chopped | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cheddar cheese, shredded | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh chives, snipped (green parts only) | 🟢 |
| to taste | Salt | 🟢 |
| to taste | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 slices | Gluten-free bread, toasted (for serving) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Crack the eggs into a small bowl. Add a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk vigorously with a fork until the yolks and whites are fully combined and slightly frothy — about 30 seconds.
2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. Add the butter and let it melt slowly. Butter should foam gently but not brown.
3. Add the chopped spinach and cook 1 minute, stirring, until wilted.
4. Pour the whisked eggs into the pan. Let them sit undisturbed for 20 seconds, then begin gently pushing the cooked egg from the edges toward the center with a silicone spatula.
5. Continue pushing and folding every 15-20 seconds until the eggs are mostly set but still look soft and glossy — about 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat while they're still slightly undercooked (they'll finish cooking in the residual heat).
6. Sprinkle the cheddar cheese over the eggs and gently fold it in until melted.
7. Top with snipped chives. Serve alongside gluten-free toast.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child snip the chives with child-safe scissors and sprinkle the cheese over the eggs.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Low heat is the secret to creamy scrambled eggs. High heat makes them rubbery — and rubbery eggs are the fastest way to lose a picky eater at 7 AM.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF bread) |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains butter + cheese — sub with olive oil and omit cheese for DF version) |

### Recipe 9.3: Very Berry Smoothie Bowl

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 kid serving or 1 adult serving

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 small | Banana (firm, sliced and frozen overnight) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Frozen strawberries | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Frozen blueberries | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free milk (or almond milk) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Peanut butter (no added sugar) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |

**Toppings (choose 2-3):**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 tbsp | Chia seeds | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Pumpkin seeds | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh strawberries, sliced | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Unsweetened coconut flakes | 🟡 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup, extra drizzle | 🟡 |

**Instructions:**

1. The night before: peel the banana, slice it into rounds, place in a freezer bag, and freeze overnight.
2. In the morning: add the frozen banana, frozen strawberries, frozen blueberries, lactose-free milk, rolled oats, peanut butter, and maple syrup to a blender.
3. Blend on low for 15 seconds, then high for 30-45 seconds. Stop and scrape down the sides with a spatula. The mixture should be thick — thicker than a drinkable smoothie. If it's too thick, add milk 1 tablespoon at a time. If too thin, add a few ice cubes.
4. Pour into a bowl. Let your child arrange the toppings on top.
5. Serve immediately with a spoon.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child arrange the toppings on top of the smoothie bowl. This is the fun part — they can make patterns, faces, or "rainbow stripes."

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Freeze bananas in single-layer slices, not whole. A frozen whole banana destroys blender blades and creates a chunky mess. Slices blend smoothly every time.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at this portion size) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use almond milk, omit peanut butter if needed — sub with sunflower seed butter) |

### Recipe 9.4: Peanut Butter & Banana Breakfast Wraps

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 5 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 2 wraps (serves 2 kids or 1 hungry adult)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 large | Corn tortillas (6-inch) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Peanut butter (no added sugar) | 🟢 |
| 1 medium | Banana (firm, sliced lengthwise into strips) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Strawberries, thinly sliced | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Butter (for cooking) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup (optional drizzle) | 🟡 |

**Instructions:**

1. Lay the corn tortillas flat on a clean surface. Warm them in a dry skillet for 15 seconds per side to make them pliable — this prevents cracking when rolling.
2. Spread 1 tablespoon of peanut butter across the center of each tortilla, leaving a 1-inch border at the edges.
3. Place banana strips and strawberry slices in a line down the center of each tortilla. Drizzle with maple syrup if using.
4. Fold the bottom edge up over the filling, then fold in the sides, and roll tightly from the bottom to form a wrap.
5. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add the butter and let it melt.
6. Place the wraps seam-side down in the skillet. Cook 2 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy.
7. Remove from the pan, let cool 1 minute, then slice diagonally in half.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child spread the peanut butter onto the tortillas and arrange the banana and strawberry pieces.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Warm corn tortillas in a dry pan before filling — cold tortillas crack and fall apart. Also: check your peanut butter label for added sugar or honey (many commercial brands add both).

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (corn tortillas are naturally GF — verify brand) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (sub butter with coconut oil) |

### Recipe 9.5: 2-Minute Microwave Oatmeal with Strawberries

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes | **Cook Time:** 2 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 serving

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Gluten-free rolled oats (not instant) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Lactose-free milk (or water) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Fresh strawberries, chopped | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Peanut butter (or 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ¼ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a microwave-safe bowl (use a larger bowl than you think you need — oatmeal bubbles up), combine the oats and milk.
2. Microwave on high for 1 minute. Stir. Microwave another 45-60 seconds until the oatmeal is thick and creamy. Watch closely — oatmeal can bubble over.
3. Remove from microwave and stir in the cinnamon.
4. Top with chopped strawberries, a dollop of peanut butter, and a drizzle of maple syrup.
5. Let cool for 1-2 minutes before serving to a child (oatmeal holds heat longer than you expect).

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child chop the strawberries with a butter knife (for older kids) or tear them with their fingers (for younger kids). They can also stir in the cinnamon.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Make a batch of "oatmeal packs" on Sunday: portion dry oats into zip-top bags with cinnamon. Grab one each morning, add milk, microwave. Saves 30 seconds of measuring on chaotic mornings.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ½ cup oats serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use water or almond milk, omit peanut butter or use seed butter) |

### Recipe 10.1: Low-FODMAP French Toast with Warm Berry Syrup

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 slices (serves 2-3 kids or 2 adults)

**Ingredients:**

For the French toast:

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 slices | Gluten-free bread (thick-cut, slightly stale is best) | 🟢 |
| 3 large | Eggs | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free milk | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Butter (for cooking) | 🟢 |
| Pinch | Salt | 🟢 |

For the warm berry syrup:

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Frozen mixed berries (strawberries + blueberries) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 1 tsp | Lemon juice | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Cornstarch mixed with 2 tsp water (slurry) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. **Make the berry syrup first.** In a small saucepan, combine frozen berries, maple syrup, and lemon juice. Heat over medium-low, stirring occasionally, until berries break down (5-7 minutes). Stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook 1 minute until thickened. Remove from heat and set aside.
2. In a wide, shallow bowl, whisk together eggs, lactose-free milk, cinnamon, vanilla, and salt until smooth and slightly frothy.
3. Heat a large griddle or non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add 1 tablespoon of butter and let it melt.
4. Working one slice at a time: dip each bread slice into the egg mixture for 10 seconds per side. Let excess drip off — soggy bread = soggy French toast.
5. Place dipped bread on the hot griddle. Cook 3-4 minutes per side, until golden brown and cooked through. The center should spring back when pressed.
6. Repeat with remaining slices, adding more butter as needed.
7. Serve 1-2 slices per person, topped with the warm berry syrup.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child whisk the egg mixture and dip the bread slices (supervised — it's a sensory experience they love). They can also drizzle the syrup on top.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Day-old or lightly toasted bread absorbs the egg custard better than fresh bread. If your GF bread is fresh, toast it lightly first, then let it cool before dipping.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF bread) |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains butter + milk — sub with coconut oil and oat milk for DF version) |

### Recipe 10.2: Bacon & Cheddar Breakfast Muffins

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 18 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6 muffins (serves 2-3 kids or makes great leftovers)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 slices | Bacon (uncured, no added sugar/honey) | 🟢 |
| 4 large | Eggs | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free milk | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cheddar cheese, shredded | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Gluten-free all-purpose flour | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives, snipped (green parts only) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Grease a 6-cup muffin tin with butter or line with silicone muffin cups.
2. Cook the bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy, about 6-8 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel to drain. Once cool, crumble into small pieces.
3. In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs until frothy. Add the lactose-free milk and whisk to combine.
4. Add the gluten-free flour, salt, and pepper. Whisk until smooth with no lumps.
5. Fold in the crumbled bacon, cheddar cheese, and snipped chives.
6. Divide the batter evenly among the 6 muffin cups (they'll be about ¾ full).
7. Bake for 16-18 minutes, until the tops are golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
8. Let cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child snip the chives with child-safe scissors, sprinkle the cheese into the batter, and help fill the muffin cups (supervised — the batter will still be warm).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These muffins reheat beautifully. Make a double batch on Saturday, refrigerate half, and you have Monday and Tuesday's breakfast solved. Reheat in a toaster oven or microwave for 20 seconds.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF flour) |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains cheese + milk — sub with DF cheese and oat milk, but texture will differ) |

### Recipe 10.3: Fluffy Low-FODMAP Waffles

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 12-15 minutes (varies by waffle iron)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4-5 square waffles (serves 2-3 kids or 2 adults)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Gluten-free all-purpose flour blend (with xanthan gum) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Sugar | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Baking powder (aluminum-free) | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Lactose-free milk | 🟢 |
| 2 large | Eggs, separated | 🟢 |
| 3 tbsp | Butter, melted and cooled (plus extra for greasing) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |

**Optional toppings:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Fresh strawberries, sliced | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Blueberries | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 2 tbsp | Lactose-free whipped cream | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat your waffle iron according to manufacturer instructions. (Tip: a medium heat setting gives the crispiest results.)
2. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the lactose-free milk, egg yolks, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir until just combined — a few small lumps are fine. Over-mixing makes tough waffles.
5. In a clean, dry bowl (use a metal or glass bowl, not plastic), beat the egg whites with a hand mixer on high speed until stiff peaks form — about 2-3 minutes. The whites should stand up straight when you lift the beaters.
6. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the batter using a rubber spatula. Use a cutting motion — fold, rotate the bowl, fold again. Don't stir or you'll deflate the air. Stop when no white streaks remain.
7. Lightly grease the preheated waffle iron with butter. Pour about ⅓ cup of batter onto the center of the iron (adjust for your waffle iron size). Close the lid and cook until golden and crisp, about 4-5 minutes.
8. Remove waffle with a fork (careful — steam is hot). Repeat with remaining batter.
9. Serve immediately with fresh berries and a drizzle of maple syrup.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** This is a great recipe for teaching kitchen skills. Let your child measure the dry ingredients, crack the eggs (into a separate bowl first — no shells), and pour the batter onto the waffle iron (adult handles the hot iron).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Separating the eggs and whipping the whites is the difference between good waffles and GREAT waffles. It adds 3 minutes of work but gives you bakery-level fluffy waffles. Freeze extras in a zip-top bag and toast them for a quick weekday breakfast.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF flour blend) |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains butter + milk — sub with coconut oil and oat milk) |

### Recipe 10.4: Potato & Cheddar Breakfast Hash

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** Serves 2 adults or 3-4 kids

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 3 medium | Yukon Gold or red potatoes, diced into ½-inch cubes | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Carrot, diced | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Zucchini, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| 4 large | Eggs | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cheddar cheese, shredded | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives or green scallion tops, chopped | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Paprika | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Place the diced potatoes in a microwave-safe bowl with 2 tablespoons of water. Cover and microwave for 3-4 minutes, until slightly tender. Drain well. (This par-cooking step saves 10 minutes of skillet time.)
2. Heat olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the par-cooked potatoes in a single layer. Cook undisturbed for 4-5 minutes, until golden brown on the bottom.
3. Flip the potatoes. Add the bell pepper, carrot, and zucchini. Cook 5-6 more minutes, stirring occasionally, until all vegetables are tender and potatoes are crispy.
4. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika. Stir to combine.
5. Make 4 small wells in the hash. Crack an egg into each well.
6. Cover the skillet with a lid and reduce heat to medium-low. Cook 4-6 minutes, depending on how you like your eggs — 4 minutes for runny yolks, 6 for set yolks.
7. Remove from heat. Sprinkle cheddar cheese over the top and let it melt for 1 minute.
8. Garnish with chives and serve directly from the skillet (it stays warmer longer).

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child help wash and scrub the potatoes (they love the sensory feel), use a kid-safe chopper for the bell pepper, and sprinkle the cheese and chives on top.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** This is the perfect "use up the vegetables" recipe. Any low-FODMAP vegetables work — swap in green beans, kale, or leftover roasted vegetables. Just avoid onion and garlic.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains cheese — omit or use DF cheese for DF version) |

### Recipe 10.5: Make-Ahead Maple Pecan Granola

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 25 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP (at ⅓ cup serving)
**Yield:** approximately 4 cups (8 servings)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 cups | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Pecans, roughly chopped | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Pumpkin seeds | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Unsweetened coconut flakes | 🟡 |
| 3 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 3 tbsp | Coconut oil, melted | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |

**For serving (per bowl):**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Lactose-free plain yogurt | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh blueberries | 🟢 |
| ⅓ cup | Prepared granola | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat the oven to 325°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, combine the oats, pecans, pumpkin seeds, and coconut flakes.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt.
4. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients. Stir with a rubber spatula until every oat and nut is coated. This takes more stirring than you think — check the bottom of the bowl for dry spots.
5. Spread the mixture in a thin, even layer on the prepared baking sheet. Press down slightly with a spatula — this helps create clusters.
6. Bake for 10 minutes. Remove from the oven, stir gently, and spread back into an even layer. Bake another 10-12 minutes until golden brown. Watch closely in the last 2-3 minutes — granola can go from golden to burnt very fast.
7. Remove from the oven. Let cool completely on the baking sheet (do not stir while cooling — this is what creates crunchy clusters).
8. Once completely cool, break into clusters and transfer to an airtight container. Store at room temperature for up to 2 weeks.

To serve: layer ½ cup yogurt, ¼ cup blueberries, and ⅓ cup granola in a bowl or jar.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure and pour the dry ingredients, stir the mixture (this is hard work — they'll feel accomplished), and press the granola onto the baking sheet.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** This granola costs a fraction of store-bought and you control every ingredient (no hidden garlic powder, onion powder, or high-FODMAP sweeteners). Double the batch and store half in the freezer — it keeps for 3 months.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ⅓ cup serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes when served without yogurt; use DF yogurt alternative if desired |
