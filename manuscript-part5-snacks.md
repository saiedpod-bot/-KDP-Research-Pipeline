# Snacks & Appetizers

*Snacks are the unsung heroes of the Low-FODMAP diet. They're what gets a hungry kid through the 4 PM meltdown, what goes in the lunchbox as a surprise, and what makes a birthday party feel normal. These recipes are designed for grab-and-go convenience, packable portability, and serious crowd appeal.*

## Chapter 17: After-School Snacks

*These snacks come together quickly and satisfy the post-school hunger that hits before dinner. They're designed to be made ahead or assembled in minutes.*

### Recipe 17.1: Peanut Butter Energy Balls

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Chill Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 12 balls (6 servings of 2 balls each)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Peanut butter (no added sugar) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ¼ cup | Mini dairy-free chocolate chips | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Unsalted peanuts, crushed (optional) | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a large bowl, combine all ingredients. Stir until fully incorporated and the mixture holds together when pressed.
2. If the mixture is too dry, add 1-2 tablespoons warm water. If too wet, add 1-2 tablespoons oat flour.
3. Line a baking sheet or plate with parchment paper.
4. Scoop 1-tablespoon portions and roll into balls between your palms.
5. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes until firm.

**Storage:** Keep in the fridge for up to 2 weeks — if they last that long.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure and pour all ingredients, stir the mixture (it's a workout), and roll the balls.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These are portable energy for after-school activities. Pop two in a small container for the ride to soccer practice. The protein and healthy fats prevent the 4 PM blood sugar crash.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at 2 balls) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use DF chocolate chips) |

### Recipe 17.2: Banana Oat Snack Bars

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 9 bars

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 medium | Bananas (firm, not overripe), mashed | 🟢 |
| 1½ cups | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Peanut butter | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ½ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Blueberries (fresh or frozen) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line an 8×8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving overhang on two sides.
2. In a large bowl, mash the bananas with a fork until smooth.
3. Add oats, peanut butter, maple syrup, cinnamon, and salt. Stir until fully combined.
4. Gently fold in the blueberries.
5. Transfer the mixture to the prepared pan. Press it into an even layer using the back of a spatula or your hands (damp hands prevent sticking).
6. Bake 18-20 minutes until golden and firm to the touch.
7. Let cool completely in the pan. Use the parchment overhang to lift out the slab. Cut into 9 bars.

**Storage:** Keep in the fridge for up to 1 week, or freeze individually wrapped for up to 3 months.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child mash the bananas, stir the mixture, press the batter into the pan (kids love this), and help cut the bars with a butter knife.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These bars are naturally sweetened by bananas and maple syrup — no refined sugar. Pack one in a lunchbox as a treat that's actually good for them.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 17.3: Rice Cake "Mini Pizzas"

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** None (or 3 minutes under broiler)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 mini pizzas (1 kid serving)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 | Brown rice cakes (plain, salted) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Low-FODMAP tomato sauce (crushed tomatoes + oregano, no garlic/onion) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Mozzarella cheese, shredded (lactose-free) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Mini pepperoni (check label — no garlic/onion/honey) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Place rice cakes on a baking sheet or microwave-safe plate.
2. Spread 1 tablespoon of tomato sauce on each rice cake, leaving a small border.
3. Top with mozzarella, pepperoni, and a sprinkle of oregano.
4. Option A (cold): Serve as-is for a lunchbox snack.
5. Option B (warm): Broil on high for 2-3 minutes until cheese is bubbly. Let cool 2 minutes before serving (rice cakes hold heat).

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child spread the sauce, sprinkle the cheese, and arrange the pepperoni on top. This is a perfect "make your own snack" activity.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Rice cakes get soft when topped with wet sauce, so pack the sauce and toppings separately for lunchboxes and let your child assemble at school. For at-home snacking, broiling makes them satisfyingly crispy.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|

| Gluten-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 17.5: Yogurt & Berry Popsicles

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Freeze Time:** 4+ hours
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6 popsicles

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1½ cups | Lactose-free plain yogurt | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Fresh strawberries, chopped | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Fresh blueberries | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a blender, combine yogurt, strawberries, blueberries, maple syrup, and vanilla. Blend until smooth.
2. Pour into popsicle molds. If you don't have molds, use small paper cups with wooden sticks inserted after 1 hour of freezing.
3. Freeze 4+ hours or overnight until solid.
4. To remove: run the outside of the mold under warm water for 15 seconds, then gently pull the stick.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash the berries, add ingredients to the blender (supervised), and pour the mixture into the molds.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These are a huge hit with kids and a clever way to get probiotics into a low-FODMAP diet. Lactose-free yogurt has the same beneficial bacteria as regular yogurt without the FODMAP trigger.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Use DF coconut yogurt instead |

### Recipe 17.6: Three-Flavor Roasted Pumpkin Seeds

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1½ cups (6 servings of ¼ cup)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1½ cups | Raw pumpkin seeds (pepitas), shelled | 🟢 |
| 1½ tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt (divided) | 🟢 |

**Flavor A — Cinnamon Maple:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |

**Flavor B — Smoky Paprika:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ tsp | Smoked paprika | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Cumin | 🟢 |

**Flavor C — Simple Salted:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| — | Olive oil + salt only | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a bowl, toss pumpkin seeds with olive oil and salt.
3. Divide the seeds into 3 bowls. Toss each with its flavoring.
4. Spread each flavor on a separate section of the baking sheet (keep them separated so flavors don't mix).
5. Roast 12-15 minutes, stirring once, until golden and fragrant. Watch closely in the last 2 minutes — seeds burn quickly.
6. Let cool completely before storing (they get crispier as they cool).

**Storage:** Airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 weeks.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child divide the seeds into bowls, sprinkle the seasonings, and toss to coat.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Pumpkin seeds are a nutritional powerhouse — packed with magnesium, zinc, and healthy fats. They're naturally low-FODMAP and make an excellent crunchy snack that satisfies the "chip craving."

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ¼ cup serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

## Chapter 18: Party Food & Game-Day Snacks

### Recipe 18.1: Low-FODMAP Mini "Pizzas" (GF Crust)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 8 mini pizzas (4 kid servings of 2 each)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Gluten-free all-purpose flour blend | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Baking powder | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free plain yogurt | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Low-FODMAP tomato sauce | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Lactose-free mozzarella, shredded | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Mini pepperoni (no garlic/onion) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a bowl, combine gluten-free flour, baking powder, and salt.
3. Add yogurt and olive oil. Stir until a dough forms. Knead gently 3-4 times on a lightly floured surface.
4. Divide dough into 8 equal pieces. Roll each into a ball, then flatten into a 4-inch round on the prepared baking sheet.
5. Bake crusts for 5 minutes (this prevents soggy bottoms).
6. Remove from oven. Top each crust with sauce, cheese, pepperoni, and oregano.
7. Return to oven and bake 8-10 minutes, until cheese is bubbly and edges are golden.
8. Let cool 5 minutes before serving.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child roll the dough balls, flatten them into rounds, and add the toppings. This is a perfect party activity — set up a "pizza bar" and let each kid top their own.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** The 2-ingredient dough (yogurt + flour) is a game-changer for gluten-free cooking. It's pliable, easy to work with, and doesn't taste like cardboard. Pre-baking the crust for 5 minutes is essential — it prevents the dreaded soggy gluten-free pizza.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF flour) |
| Dairy-Free | Use DF yogurt and DF cheese shreds |

### Recipe 18.2: Baked Chicken "Finger" Strips

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 18 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings (about 16 strips)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1½ lbs | Boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into strips | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Gluten-free panko breadcrumbs | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Parmesan cheese, grated | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Paprika | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 large | Eggs, beaten | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil (for drizzling) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Place a wire rack on a baking sheet (this makes the strips crispy on all sides).
2. In a shallow bowl, combine gluten-free panko, parmesan, paprika, salt, and pepper.
3. In another shallow bowl, beat the eggs.
4. Working one strip at a time: dip chicken in egg (let excess drip off), then press into the panko mixture, turning to coat all sides.
5. Place coated strips on the wire rack. Repeat with all strips.
6. Drizzle or spray the strips with olive oil (this is what makes them crispy instead of pale).
7. Bake 16-18 minutes, until golden brown and cooked through (165°F internal).
8. Let cool 3 minutes before serving.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child set up the "dipping station" (egg bowl, panko bowl) and help coat the chicken strips. It's messy, tactile fun.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These rival any restaurant chicken finger and freeze beautifully. Bake a double batch, cool completely, and freeze in a single layer on a baking sheet. Transfer to a freezer bag. Reheat in a 400°F oven for 8 minutes straight from frozen.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use GF panko) |
| Dairy-Free | Omit parmesan; add extra 2 tbsp GF panko |

### Recipe 18.3: Loaded Potato Skins (Low-FODMAP)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 50 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 8 potato skin halves (4 kid servings)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 medium | Russet potatoes, scrubbed | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cheddar cheese, shredded | 🟢 |
| 4 slices | Bacon (uncured, no sugar/honey), cooked and crumbled | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Lactose-free sour cream | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives, snipped | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Prick potatoes with a fork and place directly on the oven rack. Bake 40 minutes until tender.
2. Let potatoes cool 10 minutes until safe to handle. Cut each in half lengthwise.
3. Scoop out the flesh leaving a ¼-inch shell (reserve the flesh for another use — mashed potatoes tomorrow).
4. Brush the potato skins inside and out with olive oil. Sprinkle with salt.
5. Place skins cut-side up on a baking sheet. Bake 10 minutes until crispy.
6. Remove from oven. Fill each skin with cheese and crumbled bacon.
7. Return to oven and bake 3-5 minutes until cheese is melted.
8. Top with a dollop of lactose-free sour cream and snipped chives.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child scoop the potato flesh with a spoon, brush the skins with oil, and sprinkle the cheese and bacon.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Russet potatoes make the crispiest skins because they're starchier than Yukon Golds. If you're short on time, microwave the potatoes for 8 minutes instead of baking — but baking gives a better texture.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Omit cheese and sour cream; use DF alternatives |

### Recipe 18.4: Low-FODMAP Nachos with Salsa

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 bag (8 oz) | Corn tortilla chips (plain — check no garlic/onion powder) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Lactose-free cheddar cheese, shredded | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Cooked chicken or ground beef, seasoned with cumin + paprika | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free sour cream | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh chives, snipped | 🟢 |

**For the simple salsa:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 4 medium | Roma tomatoes, diced | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh cilantro, chopped | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Lime juice | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Spread tortilla chips in a single layer on the baking sheet.
3. Top with shredded cheese and cooked seasoned meat.
4. Bake 6-8 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly.
5. While the nachos bake, make the salsa: combine diced tomatoes, cilantro, lime juice, and salt in a bowl. Stir to combine.
6. Remove nachos from oven. Dollop with sour cream and spoon salsa over the top.
7. Garnish with chives and serve immediately.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child arrange the chips on the baking sheet, sprinkle the cheese, dice the tomatoes with a butter knife, and add the toppings.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** The key to Low-FODMAP nachos is a simple garlic-free, onion-free salsa. Most jarred salsas contain onion and garlic powder. Making your own takes 3 minutes and tastes significantly better.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (verify tortilla chips) |
| Dairy-Free | Omit cheese and sour cream; use DF alternatives |

### Recipe 18.5: Veggie Sticks with Creamy Avocado Dip

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ | Avocado (firm-ripe) | 🟡 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free plain yogurt | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh lime juice | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh cilantro | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Assorted veggie sticks (carrot, cucumber, bell pepper) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Halve the avocado, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a blender or food processor.
2. Add yogurt, lime juice, cilantro, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
3. If too thick, add water 1 tablespoon at a time until dipping consistency.
4. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve with veggie sticks.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child scoop the avocado into the blender, squeeze the lime juice, and arrange the veggie sticks on a plate.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Avocado is a yellow-light food — ⅛ avocado per serving is the low-FODMAP limit. This recipe uses ½ avocado across 4 servings, which is right at the safe portion if each person uses about ¼ of the dip. Stick to the portion and this is fine.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ⅛ avocado per serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Use DF yogurt (coconut-based) |

### Recipe 18.6: Turkey & Cheese Skewers

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 8 skewers (4 kid servings of 2 each)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 8 | Short skewers (6-inch, or use food picks) | 🟢 |
| 16 pieces | Oven-roasted turkey deli meat, folded into squares | 🟢 |
| 16 | Cubed cheddar cheese (½-inch cubes) | 🟢 |
| 8 | Cherry tomatoes | 🟢 |
| 16 | Cucumber rounds | 🟢 |
| 8 | Black olives | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Thread ingredients onto each skewer in this order: cucumber round → turkey square → cheese cube → tomato → olive → cucumber round.
2. Repeat until all skewers are assembled.
3. Arrange on a platter and serve.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** This is the ultimate kid-friendly party food. Let your child thread the ingredients onto the skewers in any pattern they like. It's like edible art.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Use blunt-end skewers or short food picks (available at party supply stores) for younger children. These skewers are naturally low-FODMAP, naturally gluten-free, and require no cooking. They're the perfect "I need to bring something to the party" contribution.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
