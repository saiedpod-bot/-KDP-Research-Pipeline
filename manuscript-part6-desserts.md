### Recipe 19.1: Low-FODMAP Vanilla Birthday Cake

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes | **Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 two-layer 8-inch cake (12 servings)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2½ cups | Gluten-free all-purpose flour blend (with xanthan gum) | 🟢 |
| 1½ cups | White sugar | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Baking powder | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Lactose-free whole milk, at room temperature | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Unsalted butter, softened | 🟢 |
| 3 large | Eggs, at room temperature | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |

**For the buttercream frosting:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Unsalted butter, softened | 🟢 |
| 4 cups | Powdered sugar | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Lactose-free milk | 🟢 |
| 2 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| Food coloring (optional) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease two 8-inch round cake pans and line the bottoms with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
3. Add the milk, softened butter, eggs, and vanilla. Beat with a hand mixer on medium speed for 2 minutes until smooth and fluffy. The batter will be thicker than regular cake batter — gluten-free batters are always thicker.
4. Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared pans. Smooth the tops.
5. Bake 28-32 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely.
6. While the cake cools, make the frosting. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter with a hand mixer until creamy (2 minutes). Gradually add the powdered sugar, 1 cup at a time, beating well after each addition. Add the milk and vanilla. Beat on high for 3-4 minutes until light and fluffy. Add food coloring if desired.
7. Once cakes are completely cool, place one layer on a serving plate. Spread ½ cup frosting on top. Place the second layer on top. Frost the top and sides of the entire cake.
8. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to set the frosting before slicing.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the dry ingredients, add the wet ingredients to the bowl, and — of course — lick the spatula clean (no raw egg in the frosting).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Room-temperature ingredients are critical for gluten-free baking. Cold butter or cold milk will make the cake dense and gummy. Leave your butter, eggs, and milk on the counter for 30 minutes before starting.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF flour blend) |
| Dairy-Free | Sub butter with vegan butter, milk with oat milk |

### Recipe 19.2: Chocolate Avocado Pudding

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Chill Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 | Very ripe avocados (about ⅛ per serving) | 🟡 |
| ¼ cup | Unsweetened cocoa powder | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ¼ cup | Lactose-free milk (or almond milk) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| Pinch | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Halve the avocados, remove the pits, and scoop the flesh into a food processor or blender.
2. Add cocoa powder, maple syrup, milk, vanilla, and salt.
3. Blend until completely smooth, scraping down the sides as needed (2-3 minutes).
4. Taste and adjust sweetness if desired.
5. Spoon into 4 small ramekins or cups. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes before serving.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child scoop the avocado flesh, add all ingredients to the blender, and spoon the pudding into the cups.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** This pudding tastes like rich chocolate mousse. The avocado provides creaminess and healthy fats — your child will have no idea there's a vegetable in their dessert. Use the ripest avocados you can find; underripe avocados make the pudding grainy.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ½ avocado across 4 servings) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use almond milk) |

### Recipe 19.3: "Nice" Cream — Banana Strawberry Ice Cream

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Freeze Time:** 4+ hours (bananas) + 5 minutes (blending)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 3 medium | Bananas (firm, peeled, sliced, and frozen) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Frozen strawberries | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Lactose-free milk (or almond milk) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Maple syrup (optional) | 🟡 |

**Instructions:**

1. Peel and slice the bananas. Place slices in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Freeze at least 4 hours (or overnight).
2. Add frozen banana slices, frozen strawberries, milk, and vanilla to a food processor. If using, add maple syrup.
3. Blend until smooth, scraping down the sides every 30 seconds. The mixture will look crumbly, then suddenly turn creamy — that's the "nice cream" magic. This takes 3-5 minutes.
4. Serve immediately for soft-serve consistency, or transfer to a freezer-safe container and freeze 1-2 hours for scoopable ice cream.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child peel the bananas, arrange the slices on the baking sheet, and help push the buttons on the food processor.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Frozen bananas are the base for endless flavor variations. Try chocolate (add 2 tbsp cocoa powder), peanut butter (add 2 tbsp PB), or tropical (add frozen pineapple chunks — careful: pineapple is yellow-light at ½ cup).

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 19.4: Berry Fruit Crisp with Oat Topping

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6 servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 3 cups | Frozen mixed berries (strawberries + blueberries) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 1 tbsp | Cornstarch | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Lemon juice | 🟢 |

**For the oat topping:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Gluten-free oat flour (blend oats in blender) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ¼ cup | Coconut oil, melted (or butter) | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| Pinch | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
2. In a 9×9-inch baking dish (or 6 individual ramekins), combine frozen berries, maple syrup, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Toss to coat.
3. In a medium bowl, combine oats, oat flour, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, cinnamon, and salt. Stir until the mixture forms clumps.
4. Sprinkle the oat topping evenly over the berry mixture.
5. Bake 28-30 minutes, until berries are bubbly and topping is golden brown.
6. Let cool 10 minutes before serving (the filling is lava-hot straight out of the oven).
7. Serve plain or with a scoop of lactose-free vanilla ice cream.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child toss the berries with the maple syrup and cornstarch, stir the oat topping, and sprinkle it over the berries.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** This crisp is naturally sweetened with maple syrup — no refined sugar. The cornstarch thickens the berry juices so the filling isn't watery. Serve warm with a dollop of lactose-free whipped cream for a truly impressive dessert.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ½ cup berry serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use coconut oil) |

### Recipe 19.5: Low-FODMAP Cupcakes with Strawberry Frosting

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes | **Cook Time:** 18 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 12 cupcakes

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1½ cups | Gluten-free all-purpose flour blend | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | White sugar | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Unsalted butter, softened | 🟢 |
| 2 large | Eggs, room temperature | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free milk | 🟢 |
| 1½ tsp | Baking powder | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**For the strawberry frosting:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Freeze-dried strawberries, crushed to powder | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Unsalted butter, softened | 🟢 |
| 3 cups | Powdered sugar | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Lactose-free milk | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
2. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar with a hand mixer until light and fluffy (3 minutes).
3. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each. Add vanilla.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk together gluten-free flour, baking powder, and salt.
5. Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture in three additions, alternating with the milk. Start and end with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined — don't overmix.
6. Divide the batter evenly among the 12 cupcake liners (fill each about ⅔ full).
7. Bake 16-18 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Cool in the pan 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
8. Make the frosting: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter with a hand mixer until creamy (2 minutes). Add the powdered sugar, milk, vanilla, and crushed strawberry powder. Beat on high for 3-4 minutes until fluffy and pink.
9. Once cupcakes are completely cool, frost with strawberry buttercream.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child line the muffin tin with paper liners, measure the dry ingredients, and crush the freeze-dried strawberries into powder (seal the bag and let them smash it with a rolling pin — very satisfying).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Freeze-dried strawberries (not regular dried strawberries) are the secret to intense strawberry flavor without excess moisture. You'll find them in the produce section or bulk bins. Crush them into a fine powder for frosting that's naturally colored and flavored.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF flour) |
| Dairy-Free | Sub butter with vegan butter, milk with oat milk |

### Recipe 20.1: Peanut Butter & Oat Cookies (No Flour)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 12 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 16 cookies

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Peanut butter (natural, no added sugar) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 1 large | Egg | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Baking soda | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Dairy-free chocolate chips (optional) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, combine peanut butter, maple syrup, egg, and vanilla. Stir until smooth.
3. Add baking soda and salt. Stir to combine.
4. Fold in chocolate chips if using.
5. Scoop 1-tablespoon portions and roll into balls. Place on the prepared baking sheet, spacing 2 inches apart.
6. Flatten each ball with a fork in a crisscross pattern (the classic peanut butter cookie look).
7. Bake 10-12 minutes, until edges are golden and centers look slightly underdone.
8. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes (they're fragile when hot), then transfer to a wire rack.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the peanut butter, stir the dough, roll the balls, and press the fork crisscross pattern — this is the most fun part.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These cookies are naturally gluten-free and dairy-free. They spread while baking, so leave space between each. The cookies will be soft when they come out of the oven — they firm up as they cool.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at 1-2 cookies) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 20.2: Chocolate-Dipped Strawberries

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Chill Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 12 chocolate-dipped strawberries (6 servings of 2 each)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 12 large | Fresh strawberries, washed and dried completely | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Dairy-free dark chocolate chips (70%+ cacao) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Coconut oil | 🟢 |

**Optional toppings:**
- Crushed peanuts 🟢
- Unsweetened coconut flakes 🟡
- Sea salt flakes 🟢

**Instructions:**

1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Ensure strawberries are completely dry (any water will seize the chocolate).
2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine chocolate chips and coconut oil. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until fully melted and smooth (about 1½-2 minutes total).
3. Holding a strawberry by the stem or leaves, dip it into the melted chocolate, letting excess drip back into the bowl.
4. Place on the parchment-lined sheet. Sprinkle with optional toppings before the chocolate sets.
5. Repeat with all strawberries.
6. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes until chocolate is firm.
7. Serve cold or at room temperature.

**Storage:** Keep in the fridge for up to 3 days (they're best within 24 hours).

**👶 Little Chef Task:** This is the most fun recipe in the book. Let your child wash and dry the strawberries, dip them in the chocolate, and add the toppings. Yes, it gets messy. Yes, it's worth it.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Dark chocolate (70%+ cacao) is naturally low in FODMAPs because the high cocoa content means less sugar and milk solids. Milk chocolate is not safe in standard amounts. The coconut oil helps the chocolate set with a glossy, snappy finish.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at 2 strawberries) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use DF dark chocolate) |

### Recipe 20.3: 3-Ingredient Banana Oat Cookies

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 12 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 10 cookies

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 medium | Bananas (firm, not overripe), mashed | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Peanut butter (or sunbutter) | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Cinnamon (optional) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a bowl, mash the bananas with a fork until smooth.
3. Add oats, peanut butter, and cinnamon (if using). Stir until fully combined.
4. Scoop 1-tablespoon portions and place on the prepared baking sheet. Flatten slightly with the back of a spoon — these cookies don't spread on their own.
5. Bake 10-12 minutes until golden and firm around the edges.
6. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child mash the bananas, stir the mixture, and scoop the cookies onto the baking sheet.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These are the simplest low-FODMAP cookies you'll ever make. Three ingredients. No sugar. No flour. No dairy. They're not photogenic but kids love them. Add 2 tablespoons of mini chocolate chips for a more indulgent version.

### Recipe 20.4: Maple Roasted Pineapple with Coconut

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 cups | Fresh pineapple, cut into 1-inch chunks | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 1 tbsp | Coconut oil, melted | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Unsweetened coconut flakes | 🟡 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a bowl, toss pineapple chunks with maple syrup and melted coconut oil.
3. Spread in a single layer on the baking sheet.
4. Roast 15 minutes. Sprinkle coconut flakes over the top. Roast 5 more minutes until pineapple is golden at the edges and coconut is toasted.
5. Let cool 5 minutes before serving. The caramelized syrup is very hot.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child toss the pineapple in the bowl, spread it on the baking sheet, and sprinkle the coconut flakes.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Roasting pineapple concentrates its natural sugars and creates a caramelized, almost candy-like treat. This satisfies the "candy craving" in a naturally low-FODMAP way. Serve warm with a scoop of lactose-free vanilla ice cream for a company-worthy dessert.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ½ cup pineapple) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 20.5: No-Bake Peanut Butter Rice Crispy Treats

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Chill Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 9 squares

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 3 cups | Crisped rice cereal (check label — plain, no HFCS or garlic) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Peanut butter (no added sugar) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ¼ cup | Coconut oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract | 🟢 |
| Pinch | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Line an 8×8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving overhang on two sides.
2. In a small saucepan over low heat, combine peanut butter, maple syrup, and coconut oil. Stir continuously until melted and smooth (about 2-3 minutes). Do not boil.
3. Remove from heat. Stir in vanilla and salt.
4. Place the crisped rice cereal in a large bowl. Pour the peanut butter mixture over the cereal. Stir gently but thoroughly until all cereal is coated.
5. Transfer to the prepared pan. Press firmly into an even layer using the back of a spatula or damp hands. (Press firmly — loosely packed treats fall apart.)
6. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes until firm.
7. Use the parchment overhang to lift the slab out. Cut into 9 squares.

**Storage:** Keep in the fridge for up to 2 weeks, or freeze for 3 months.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the cereal, pour the peanut butter mixture (adult handles the warm saucepan), and press the mixture into the pan — kids LOVE pressing things into pans.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These are a perfect lunchbox treat and a nostalgic classic made low-FODMAP. The coconut oil helps them set firmly so they don't crumble in a lunchbox. If your child's school is nut-free, use sunflower seed butter — just note that it turns green when baked (a natural reaction, completely safe).

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at 1 square) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (verify rice cereal is GF) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |
