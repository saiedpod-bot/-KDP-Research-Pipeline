# Drinks & Smoothies

*Hydration matters more than most parents realize when managing IBS. These drinks are designed to soothe, hydrate, and nourish — without triggering symptoms. They're also a great way to sneak extra nutrients into a picky eater's day.*

## Chapter 21: Gut-Healing Smoothies

*These smoothies are thick, satisfying, and designed to be gentle on sensitive digestive systems. They make an excellent breakfast, snack, or post-school refuel.*

---

### Recipe 21.1: Strawberry Banana Gut-Healer

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 kid serving (about 8 oz)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 small | Banana (firm, sliced and frozen) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Frozen strawberries | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free milk (or almond milk) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Lactose-free plain yogurt | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup (optional) | 🟡 |
| 1 tsp | Chia seeds | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Add all ingredients to a blender in the order listed (liquids first helps blending).
2. Blend on high for 30-45 seconds until smooth and creamy.
3. If too thick, add milk 1 tablespoon at a time. If too thin, add a few ice cubes.
4. Pour into a glass and serve immediately.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child add ingredients to the blender and press the blend button.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Chia seeds add fiber and omega-3s without affecting the taste. They also thicken the smoothie naturally. Soaking chia seeds for 5 minutes before blending helps them break down more smoothly.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Use almond milk + DF coconut yogurt |

### Recipe 21.2: Tropical Turmeric Smoothie

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 kid serving

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Frozen pineapple chunks | 🟢 |
| ½ small | Banana (firm, frozen) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Lactose-free milk (or canned coconut milk) | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Ground turmeric | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Fresh ginger, peeled | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| Pinch | Black pepper (activates turmeric absorption) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Add all ingredients to a blender. Liquids first.
2. Blend on high for 45-60 seconds until completely smooth.
3. Pour and serve immediately.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the ingredients and push the blender button.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** The black pepper isn't optional — it increases turmeric absorption by 2,000%. The pineapple and banana mask the earthy turmeric flavor well enough that most kids won't notice. Turmeric is a natural anti-inflammatory that can help calm an irritated gut.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at ½ cup pineapple per serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use coconut milk) |

## Chapter 22: Warm Drinks & Hydration

*These warm drinks are comforting, soothing, and designed to be gentle on a sensitive stomach. They're perfect for sick days, cold afternoons, or winding down before bed.*

---

### Recipe 22.1: Soothing Ginger & Lemon Tea

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes | **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 cup

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 inches | Fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Water | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| 1 | Cinnamon stick (optional) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Place the sliced ginger and water in a small saucepan. If using the cinnamon stick, add it now.
2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer 8-10 minutes.
3. Remove from heat. Strain into a mug.
4. Stir in lemon juice and maple syrup. Let cool to a safe drinking temperature before serving to a child.
5. Serve warm.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child peel the ginger with a spoon (the spoon edge scrapes the skin off easily) and squeeze the lemon juice.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Ginger is a natural anti-nausea remedy and gentle on the digestive system. This tea is excellent for mornings when your child's stomach is upset or for winding down in the evening. Make a larger batch and keep it in a thermos for school.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 22.2: Golden Milk (Turmeric Latte)

**Prep Time:** 3 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 cup

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Lactose-free milk (or canned coconut milk) | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Ground turmeric | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Ground ginger | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Cinnamon | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| Pinch | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Coconut oil (optional, for healthy fat) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a small saucepan, whisk together all ingredients.
2. Heat over medium heat, whisking frequently, until hot but not boiling (about 5-7 minutes).
3. Remove from heat. Pour into a mug through a fine-mesh strainer if the spices are gritty.
4. Let cool to a safe drinking temperature before serving.
5. Serve warm.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the spices and whisk the milk while it heats (adult handles the stove).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Golden milk is an anti-inflammatory powerhouse. The coconut oil and black pepper are essential for turmeric absorption (turmeric is fat-soluble, piperine in pepper boosts absorption 2,000%). If your child resists the color (it's bright yellow), call it "sunshine milk" or "magic milk."

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (use canned coconut milk) |

### Recipe 22.3: Warm Berry & Mint Infusion

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 cup

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Frozen mixed berries (strawberries + blueberries) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Water | 🟢 |
| 4-5 | Fresh mint leaves | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Maple syrup (optional) | 🟡 |
| 1 tsp | Lemon juice (optional) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a small saucepan, combine frozen berries and water.
2. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat and simmer 5 minutes.
3. Remove from heat. Add mint leaves and let steep for 3 minutes.
4. Strain into a mug, pressing the berries gently to extract all the liquid.
5. Stir in maple syrup and lemon juice if using.
6. Let cool to a safe temperature before serving.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child add the berries to the pot, tear the mint leaves, and stir in the maple syrup.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** This is a naturally sweet, caffeine-free alternative to juice or soda. The berries provide antioxidants and natural sweetness. Serve it warm in winter or chilled over ice in summer.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |
