# PART 3

# Lunchbox Solutions

*The school lunch is the daily frontier of the Low-FODMAP diet. You can't be there to remind your child about portion sizes, and your child doesn't want to be different. These recipes solve both problems — they're safe, they're packable, and they look like "normal" lunchbox food. Every recipe in this section is designed to hold up in a lunchbox for 4+ hours without refrigeration (though we recommend an ice pack).*

---

## Chapter 11: Thermos Meals (Hot Lunch)

*Hot lunch is a game-changer for kids on the Low-FODMAP diet. A thermos keeps food at safe temperatures, and it means your child isn't eating a cold sandwich while everyone else has hot lunch. These recipes are designed to reheat well and stay appetizing in a thermos until noon.*

---

### Recipe 11.1: Chicken & Rice Soup

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 6 servings (freeze individual portions for quick thermos packing)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 | Carrots, peeled and diced | 🟢 |
| 2 stalks | Celery (outer stalks only), diced | 🟢 |
| 1 lb | Boneless skinless chicken thighs (or breasts) | 🟢 |
| 6 cups | Low-FODMAP chicken broth (Pacific brand or homemade, no onion/garlic) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | White rice, uncooked | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Fresh spinach, roughly chopped | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives, snipped (green parts only) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried thyme | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add carrots and celery. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
2. Add the chicken thighs (whole), low-FODMAP broth, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes.
3. Remove chicken thighs to a cutting board. Shred with two forks. Return shredded chicken to the pot.
4. Add the uncooked rice. Simmer 15 minutes until rice is tender.
5. Stir in the spinach and cook 2 minutes until wilted.
6. Remove from heat. Stir in snipped chives.
7. Let cool slightly before filling thermos (very hot liquid can damage some thermoses and burns little mouths).

**To pack:** Warm the thermos by filling it with boiling water and letting it sit for 5 minutes. Empty, fill with hot soup, and close immediately.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash and scrub the carrots, measure the rice, and snip the chives with child-safe scissors.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Make a double batch on Sunday and freeze in individual silicone cups. Thaw overnight in the fridge, reheat, and pack. This soup is your emergency "I forgot to pack lunch" solution — keep a frozen portion ready.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 11.2: Creamy Low-FODMAP Mac and Cheese

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 cups | Gluten-free elbow pasta (brown rice or corn-based) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Butter | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Gluten-free all-purpose flour | 🟢 |
| 1½ cups | Lactose-free whole milk | 🟢 |
| 1½ cups | Sharp cheddar cheese, freshly grated | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Parmesan cheese, grated | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Paprika (optional) | 🟢 |
| Pinch | Nutmeg (optional, enhances cheese flavor) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the gluten-free pasta according to package directions until al dente. Drain and return to the pot. (Gluten-free pasta overcooks quickly — set a timer 1 minute less than the package says.)
2. While the pasta cooks, make the cheese sauce. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
3. Whisk in the gluten-free flour. Cook 1 minute, whisking constantly — this is a roux. It should smell toasty, not raw.
4. Slowly pour in the lactose-free milk while whisking continuously. Keep whisking until the mixture thickens, about 3-4 minutes. It should coat the back of a spoon.
5. Remove from heat. Add the shredded cheddar, parmesan, salt, paprika, and nutmeg. Whisk until smooth and all cheese is melted.
6. Pour the cheese sauce over the cooked pasta. Stir gently to coat every piece.
7. Let cool slightly before packing into a thermos.

**To pack:** Same hot thermos method as Recipe 11.1. Pre-warm the thermos with boiling water, then add the mac and cheese.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child measure the pasta, sprinkle the cheese into the sauce, and stir the cheese sauce (adult handles the hot stove).

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Freshly grated cheese melts smoothly. Pre-shredded cheese has anti-caking agents that make the sauce grainy. Grate a block of cheddar on Sunday and store in a zip-top bag for the week.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF pasta and flour) |
| Dairy-Free | No — sub butter with vegan butter, milk with oat milk, cheeses with DF alternatives |

### Recipe 11.3: Low-FODMAP Turkey & Quinoa Meatballs with Tomato Sauce

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 25 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 16-18 meatballs (4 kid servings)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Ground turkey (93/7) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cooked quinoa, cooled | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Egg | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Parmesan cheese, grated | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives, finely chopped | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh parsley, finely chopped | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Olive oil (for cooking) | 🟢 |

**For the quick tomato sauce:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 can (15 oz) | Crushed tomatoes (check label — no garlic/onion added) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dried basil | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Sugar (balances acidity) | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, cooked quinoa, egg, parmesan, chives, parsley, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix with your hands until just combined — over-mixing makes tough meatballs.
3. Roll into 1½-inch balls (about 1½ tablespoons each). Place on the prepared baking sheet, leaving slight space between each.
4. Bake for 18-20 minutes, until golden brown and cooked through (internal temp 165°F).
5. While meatballs bake, make the sauce. In a small saucepan, combine crushed tomatoes, olive oil, basil, sugar, and salt. Simmer over low heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
6. Add baked meatballs to the sauce and stir to coat.

**To pack:** Place 3-4 meatballs with sauce in a pre-warmed thermos. Add a side of rice or gluten-free pasta if desired.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child mix the ingredients with their hands (use gloves if they prefer not to touch raw meat) and roll the meatballs. Kids love this part.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These meatballs freeze beautifully. Bake a double batch, flash-freeze on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag. Add to sauce straight from frozen — just simmer 5 extra minutes.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains parmesan — omit or use DF parmesan) |

### Recipe 11.4: Beef & Vegetable Stir-Fry with Rice

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 12 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 lb | Beef sirloin or flank steak, thinly sliced across the grain | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Sesame oil | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh ginger, peeled and grated | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Broccoli florets (heads only, no stems) | 🟢 |
| 1 large | Red bell pepper, thinly sliced | 🟢 |
| 2 medium | Carrots, julienned or thinly sliced | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Cooked white rice (per serving) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Sesame seeds (for garnish) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, sesame oil, and grated ginger. Pour half over the sliced beef and toss to coat. Let marinate 5 minutes (or overnight in the fridge).
2. Heat olive oil in a large wok or non-stick skillet over high heat until shimmering.
3. Add the beef in a single layer (work in batches if needed — overcrowding steams instead of sears). Cook 2 minutes per side, undisturbed, until browned. Remove beef to a plate.
4. In the same pan, add broccoli florets, bell pepper, and carrots. Stir-fry 3-4 minutes until crisp-tender. Add 1 tablespoon of water if the pan looks dry and scrape up any browned bits.
5. Return the beef to the pan. Add the remaining tamari mixture. Toss everything together and cook 1 minute.
6. Remove from heat. Garnish with sesame seeds.

**To pack:** Pack rice and stir-fry separately in the thermos (layer rice at the bottom, stir-fry on top) to prevent rice from getting soggy. Or pack in a divided thermos container.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child arrange the sliced beef on the plate, measure the tamari, and sprinkle the sesame seeds on top.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Slice beef partially frozen — it's much easier to cut thin slices against the grain. "Against the grain" means cutting perpendicular to the muscle fibers, which makes the meat tender instead of chewy.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (broccoli heads only — ½ cup per serving max) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use tamari, not regular soy sauce) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

## Chapter 12: Cold Lunch Boxes

*Not every kid wants a hot lunch. These cold lunches are designed to be packed in the morning, kept cool with an ice pack, and eaten at room temperature or cold. They're colorful, appealing, and hold up well in a lunchbox.*

---

### Recipe 12.1: Chicken Salad Lettuce Wraps

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus leftover chicken) | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 wraps (2 kid servings)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 cups | Cooked chicken, shredded or diced | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Lactose-free plain yogurt (or mayo) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Red seedless grapes, quartered | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Celery (outer stalks), finely diced | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh chives, snipped | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Lemon juice | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| 4 large | Butter lettuce leaves (or romaine hearts) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a medium bowl, combine the shredded chicken, yogurt (or mayo), quartered grapes, celery, chives, lemon juice, and salt. Stir until well combined.
2. Taste and adjust salt or lemon as needed.
3. Lay the lettuce leaves flat on a clean surface. Divide the chicken salad evenly among the leaves.
4. Fold the lettuce around the filling like a taco, or roll like a wrap. Pack the filling and lettuce separately, and let your child assemble at lunch — this prevents soggy lettuce.

**To pack:** Place chicken salad in a small container. Pack lettuce leaves in a separate bag or container with a paper towel to absorb moisture. Include a spoon and instructions to "scoop and wrap."

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child shred the cooked chicken with two forks, quarter the grapes with a butter knife, and snip the chives.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Use leftover chicken from last night's dinner. Roast an extra chicken breast on Sunday specifically for lunch prep. Cold chicken shreds more easily than warm chicken.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes when using mayonnaise instead of yogurt |

### Recipe 12.2: Rainbow Quinoa Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes (quinoa)
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Quinoa, rinsed | 🟢 |
| 2 cups | Water | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Cucumber, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Cherry tomatoes, quartered | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Carrot, grated | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh parsley, chopped | 🟢 |

**For the lemon vinaigrette:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 3 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Fresh lemon juice | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dijon mustard (check label — no garlic) | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Rinse quinoa in a fine-mesh strainer (this removes bitterness). Combine quinoa and water in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15 minutes until water is absorbed and quinoa is fluffy.
2. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and let cool completely.
3. In a large bowl, combine cooled quinoa, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, bell pepper, carrot, and parsley.
4. In a small jar, combine olive oil, lemon juice, mustard, oregano, and salt. Shake well.
5. Pour the vinaigrette over the quinoa mixture. Toss to coat evenly.
6. Refrigerate until ready to pack.

**To pack:** Portion into lunch containers. This salad actually tastes better after sitting for a few hours — the flavors meld. Serve cold or at room temperature.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child rinse the quinoa, measure the vegetables, and shake the dressing in a jar with the lid securely tightened.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Make a large batch on Sunday and portion into 4 lunch containers. This salad keeps 4-5 days in the fridge and is just as good on Friday as it was on Monday.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes |

### Recipe 12.3: Turkey & Cheese Roll-Ups with Rice Cakes

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 2 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 6 slices | Oven-roasted turkey breast (deli, no added garlic/onion/honey) | 🟢 |
| 6 | Cheddar cheese sticks (string cheese), cut in half | 🟢 |
| 4 | Plain rice cakes (brown rice, salted) | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Peanut butter (or sunbutter for nut-free schools) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Strawberries, sliced | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Carrot sticks | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Lay the turkey slices flat. Place a cheese stick half at one end of each turkey slice.
2. Roll the turkey tightly around the cheese. Secure with a toothpick if needed (remind your child to remove it before eating).
3. Spread peanut butter onto two of the rice cakes. Top with sliced strawberries and the remaining rice cakes to make sandwiches. Cut into wedges.
4. Pack carrot sticks in a small container.

**To pack:** Arrange roll-ups, rice cake sandwiches, carrot sticks, and extra strawberries in a bento-style lunchbox with compartments. Add an ice pack to keep cheese cold.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child spread the peanut butter onto the rice cakes with a butter knife and arrange the strawberry slices on top.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** String cheese is naturally low-lactose (hard cheese) and a perfect FODMAP-safe protein. For nut-free classrooms, substitute sunflower seed butter — but check the label for added honey, which some brands use.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (verify rice cakes are GF) |
| Dairy-Free | No (contains cheese — omit and use turkey-only rolls for DF) |

### Recipe 12.4: Low-FODMAP Pasta Salad with Cherry Tomatoes & Basil

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 4 kid servings

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 2 cups | Gluten-free rotini or fusilli pasta | 🟢 |
| 1 cup | Cherry tomatoes, halved | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cucumber, diced (seeds removed) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Red bell pepper, diced | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Fresh basil, thinly sliced (chiffonade) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Lactose-free mozzarella cheese, cubed (optional) | 🟢 |

**For the dressing:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 3 tbsp | Olive oil | 🟢 |
| 2 tbsp | Red wine vinegar | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Dijon mustard (no garlic) | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Dried oregano | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| Pinch | Black pepper | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Cook the gluten-free pasta according to package directions. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop cooking and cool quickly.
2. While pasta cools, prepare the vegetables.
3. In a large bowl, combine cooled pasta, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, basil, and mozzarella (if using).
4. In a small jar, combine olive oil, red wine vinegar, mustard, oregano, salt, and pepper. Shake well.
5. Pour dressing over the pasta salad. Toss gently to combine.
6. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes before serving (the flavors need time to meld).

**To pack:** Portion into lunch containers. This pasta salad is best cold or at room temperature. It keeps 3-4 days in the fridge.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child halve the cherry tomatoes with a butter knife, tear the basil leaves, and shake the dressing in a jar.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Remove the seeds from the cucumber before dicing — cucumber seeds release water and make the pasta salad watery over time. Halve the cucumber lengthwise and run a spoon down the center to scoop out the seeds.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF pasta) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes when omitting mozzarella |

### Recipe 13.1: Lactose-Free Ranch Dip with Veggie Sticks

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 cup dip (8 servings of 2 tbsp each)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Lactose-free plain yogurt (or Greek-style) | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Mayonnaise | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh chives, finely chopped | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Fresh dill, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried) | 🟢 |
| 1 tsp | Lemon juice | 🟢 |
| ½ tsp | Dried parsley | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |
| ⅛ tsp | Black pepper | 🟢 |

**For dipping (choose 2-3 per lunchbox):**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| ½ cup | Carrot sticks | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cucumber rounds | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Red bell pepper strips | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Cherry tomatoes | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a small bowl, whisk together yogurt, mayonnaise, chives, dill, lemon juice, parsley, salt, and pepper.
2. Taste and adjust. Add more lemon for brightness, more salt if flat.
3. Transfer to a small container with a tight-fitting lid. Refrigerate at least 15 minutes to let flavors meld.
4. Wash and cut vegetables. Store in a container with a damp paper towel to keep crisp.

**To pack:** 2 tablespoons of ranch in a small dip container with a selection of veggie sticks.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child wash the vegetables, snip the herbs with scissors, and whisk the dip ingredients together.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** This dip keeps 5-7 days in the fridge. Make it on Sunday and portion 2 tablespoons into small containers for the week. No garlic powder, no onion powder — and your kids won't miss them.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Yes when using DF yogurt + vegan mayo |

### Recipe 13.2: Hard Cheese Cubes with Grapes

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 1 serving

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** Grapes that are still attached to the stem last longer in the fridge. Remove them from the stem only when packing lunches. Hard cheeses like cheddar, Swiss, and Monterey Jack are naturally low in lactose — safe even for kids who react to regular milk.

### Recipe 13.3: Bell Pepper "Sandwich" Bites

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** None
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 8 mini sandwiches (2 kid servings)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 large | Red bell pepper | 🟢 |
| 4 slices | Turkey or chicken deli meat (no garlic/onion/honey) | 🟢 |
| 2 slices | Hard cheese (cheddar, Swiss, or provolone) | 🟢 |
| 1 tbsp | Lactose-free cream cheese (optional, for spreading) | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. Wash the bell pepper. Slice off the top and bottom to create flat ends. Remove the seeds and membranes from inside.
2. Slice the pepper crosswise into 8 rings, about ½-inch thick each.
3. Using a small round cookie cutter or a knife, cut the turkey and cheese into circles that fit inside the pepper rings.
4. If using cream cheese, spread a thin layer inside each pepper ring.
5. Layer a turkey circle and a cheese circle inside each pepper ring.
6. Press gently so everything stays together.

**To pack:** Place in a single layer in a lunch container, separated by small pieces of parchment paper to prevent sticking.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** Let your child push the cookie cutter through the turkey and cheese slices, and layer the ingredients inside the pepper rings.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These are a brilliant low-carb, low-FODMAP alternative to sandwiches. They look fun and appetizing — kids often eat foods more readily when they're presented in an unusual shape or form.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes |
| Gluten-Free | Yes |
| Dairy-Free | Omit cheese layer; use turkey only |

### Recipe 13.4: No-Bake Peanut Butter & Oat Bites (Lunchbox Bonus)

*These double as a snack and a treat. They hold up well in a lunchbox and provide protein and fiber for sustained energy through the afternoon.*

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Chill Time:** 30 minutes
**FODMAP Rating:** 🟢 Low-FODMAP
**Yield:** 12 bites (6 kid servings of 2 bites each)

**Ingredients:**

| Qty | Ingredient | FODMAP |
|-----|------------|--------|
| 1 cup | Gluten-free rolled oats | 🟢 |
| ½ cup | Peanut butter (no added sugar or honey) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Maple syrup | 🟡 |
| ¼ cup | Unsalted peanuts, crushed (optional) | 🟢 |
| ¼ cup | Mini chocolate chips (dairy-free, optional) | 🟢 |
| ¼ tsp | Salt | 🟢 |

**Instructions:**

1. In a large bowl, combine all ingredients. Stir vigorously with a rubber spatula until everything is evenly mixed and the mixture holds together when pressed.
2. If the mixture seems too dry to hold together, add 1-2 tablespoons warm water or extra peanut butter. If too wet, add 1-2 tablespoons oat flour (blend oats in a blender).
3. Line a small baking sheet or plate with parchment paper.
4. Scoop about 1 tablespoon of mixture and roll into a ball between your palms. Place on the parchment paper.
5. Repeat until all mixture is used (about 12 bites).
6. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes until firm.

**To pack:** Place 2 bites in a small container or silicone cup. These hold up at room temperature but are best kept cool.

**👶 Little Chef Task:** This is a perfect "kid makes it" recipe. Let your child measure and pour ingredients, stir the mixture (it's a good arm workout), and roll the balls.

**👩‍🍳 Parent Tip:** These are called "energy bites" for a reason — the combination of oats, peanut butter, and maple syrup provides slow-release energy that carries kids through the afternoon slump without the sugar crash. Keep a batch in the fridge for grab-and-go snacks.

**✅ Triple-Check:**
| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Low-FODMAP | Yes (at 2 bites per serving) |
| Gluten-Free | Yes (use certified GF oats) |
| Dairy-Free | Yes (ensure chocolate chips are DF) |
